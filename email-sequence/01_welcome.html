Hey —

You signed up. That matters to me more than you probably expect, because the list you just joined is small on purpose and I would rather write to one person who actually reads than to a room that does not.

Before we go any further, a heads up on the cadence. For the next couple of weeks you are going to get more than one letter from me. Not because I have found a marketing funnel I want to run you through, but because I would rather give you more than you were expecting at the start and let it settle into something quieter after. A handful of letters up front. Then it will trickle down to one a week — a single Musing, a single question, the door left open if you want to write back.

That is the whole trade.

While you are here, a recommendation, because I think it matters when you are on a list like this. If you have not read Atomic Habits by James Clear, read it. That book found me during a stretch where I was picking up the pieces of a life I had broken myself, and I do not say that lightly. I needed something that was not advice shouted from a stage or a framework that required me to already be the kind of man I was trying to become. Atomic Habits did not ask me to believe anything grand about myself. It asked me to put the running shoes by the door. It asked me to shorten the distance between the person I said I wanted to be and the next five minutes. That was the size of change I could actually carry at the time, and it is the reason some of the things I write about now exist at all.

That is the whole letter for today.

Before I send anything else, tell me one thing — what made you subscribe? One line is enough. I read every reply.

— Ian
