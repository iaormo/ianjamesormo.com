A Musing on living here, now

Hey {{contact.first_name}},

Something I've been thinking about.

There's a specific weight to living in this country right now, and I don't think the people carrying it give themselves enough credit for carrying it. Rice is up again. The electric bill's doing that thing where you read the number twice. Brownouts still arrive on schedule. Somebody in your family's abroad, about to be abroad, or lying awake at 2 a.m. doing the math on whether abroad is the answer.

And still — people show up. Open the store at six. Ride the jeep. Pack the baon. Laugh loud enough that a stranger across the street turns to see what's funny. That resilience doesn't come from a book. It comes from generations of people who figured out how to be joyful in a place that wasn't always easy to be joyful in.

I'm not writing to romanticize anything. The pressure's real. You can't gratitude-journal your way out of it.

I just want to say — the weight you're carrying is heavier than you're giving yourself credit for. And one of the quiet acts of grace this week is to tell the truth about what it actually costs to be you.

Not to anyone who can fix it. Maybe to yourself in the kitchen while the kettle's on. To one person who loves you. To God, if you pray.

That's a starting point. Some weeks a starting point is the whole thing.

— Ian
