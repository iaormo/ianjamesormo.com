The story behind a line

There is a line I have written more than once in different forms — that my wife remained. Not stayed. Not forgave. Not accepted. Remained. It is the smallest word I could find for the largest thing a person has ever done for me.

The moment that word came out of was not dramatic. It was a morning at the kitchen table in a house we could no longer really afford, and she was pouring coffee, and she slid the cup across the table without looking up, and the whole weight of the last year was in the way she did not look up. She did not need to make a speech. She did not need to remind me what I had done. She slid the coffee and she remained.

I think a lot of people expect the grace moments in a story like mine to be big. A hand on the shoulder, a long conversation, a cinematic forgiveness scene. The real ones are almost always a cup of coffee at an angle you were not expecting to receive it.

Tell me a line — from any book, any song, anywhere — that ever did this to you. Named something you had not been able to name. I want to know.

— Ian
