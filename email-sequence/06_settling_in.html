Settling into the rhythm

Hey {{contact.first_name}},

This is the last of the opening letters.

From here, it drops to one a week. A single Musing, a single question, door left open if you want to write back. That's the rhythm from here on.

Before we settle in, I want to say something honest.

I know what it costs, in a small and accumulating way, to let one more voice into your day. I know how many lists you could be on. I know how many you've unsubscribed from this month. You let me in, {{contact.first_name}}, and I don't take it for granted.

Here's what I'll try to do from here.

One letter a week. Honest, even the weeks when honesty isn't glamorous. I won't sell you something I haven't built with my own hands. I won't manufacture urgency. If I ever have something worth your money, I'll ask for it like a human — once — and then move on.

One more thing, because I'd rather you hear it from me than guess. There's a book I've been working on for a while. It's not ready yet. When it is, you'll be the first to know. Until then, these letters are the work — and I'm trying to write them the way I wish somebody had written to me on the days I needed a letter most.

In return I'd ask only this.

When a letter lands and names something you hadn't been able to name, tell me. One line. Nothing else.

That's how I know I'm still writing to a person and not to a spreadsheet.

Thanks for being here, {{contact.first_name}}. Glad you came.

— Ian
