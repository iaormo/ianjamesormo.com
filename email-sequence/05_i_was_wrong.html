Something I got wrong

For a long time I believed that the way back from a bad season was to work harder than I had ever worked before. To out-hustle the version of myself that had failed. To prove, through sheer volume, that I was not that man anymore.

It did not work. What it did was aim the same engine that had driven me into the ditch at a different destination. Same engine. Different road. I was still outrunning a version of myself instead of becoming a different one, and the body and the marriage and the bank account all kept score in their own ways.

What actually worked was smaller and more embarrassing to admit. I started telling the truth in ordinary conversations. I stopped performing contrition and started practicing repair. I let my wife see me fail at small things without collapsing. I read slower. I prayed worse, meaning more honestly and less performatively. The rebuilding was not louder than the fall. It was quieter.

If I am still wrong about this, tell me. I would rather be corrected by a stranger who has done the work than praised by one who has not.

— Ian
