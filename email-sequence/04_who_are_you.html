A question for you

Hey {{contact.first_name}},

I want to ask you something, and I want you to answer it like nobody's gonna read it except me. Because nobody is.

Who are you, really?

Not the job title. Not the bio line. Not the version your family describes at Sunday lunch. Not the person you've built so nobody else has to worry about you.

Are you a parent still figuring out how to be a parent? A student paying your own tuition because nobody else is going to, working graveyard so you can show up to your 8 a.m.? A nurse at the end of a 12-hour shift, reading this on the jeep home?

A father trying to stop being the man who disappointed them and become the one they deserve? A mother who hasn't had a thought that was only hers in longer than you want to admit? An OFW who's been away longer than you were ever home?

A graduate doing work that has nothing to do with the degree? A teacher who loves the kids but's tired of being paid like the country doesn't love you back? A driver, a barista, a cook, a guard, a freelancer, a call-center agent on the late shift?

A guy my age trying to start over, afraid he's too late?

I want to know. Not for analytics. I write better when I know who's on the other side of the screen. And there's something about being named that makes a person feel less invisible for a minute.

Hit reply, {{contact.first_name}}. One line. Who you are, and one thing you're carrying.

I'll read it. I always do.

— Ian
